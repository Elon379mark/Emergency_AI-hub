EMERGENCY MEDICAL PROTOCOLS MANUAL
====================================
Version 1.0 - Offline Reference Guide

SECTION 1: FRACTURES AND ORTHOPEDIC INJURIES
=============================================

1.1 LEG FRACTURE PROTOCOL
--------------------------
Signs and Symptoms:
- Visible deformity or unnatural angle of the limb
- Severe pain and tenderness at injury site
- Swelling and bruising
- Inability to bear weight or move the limb
- Possible bone protruding through skin (open fracture)

Treatment Steps:
1. DO NOT attempt to straighten the fracture
2. Immobilize the leg in the position found
3. Apply a rigid splint extending above and below the fracture site
4. Pad the splint with soft material to prevent pressure sores
5. Secure the splint with bandages, not too tight to restrict circulation
6. Check circulation (pulse, color, warmth) below injury every 5 minutes
7. Apply cold pack (wrapped in cloth) to reduce swelling - 20 min on / 20 min off
8. Elevate the limb if no spinal injury suspected
9. Administer pain relief as appropriate
10. Do NOT give food or water (surgery may be needed)
11. Transport to medical facility as soon as possible

For Open Fractures:
- Cover wound with sterile dressing
- Do NOT push bone back in
- Apply gentle pressure around wound to control bleeding

1.2 ARM FRACTURE PROTOCOL
--------------------------
Treatment Steps:
1. Support the arm in a comfortable position
2. Apply a sling to immobilize
3. Use padding between arm and body
4. Check circulation in fingers regularly
5. Apply cold pack to reduce swelling

1.3 SPINAL FRACTURE PROTOCOL
------------------------------
CRITICAL - Assume spinal injury for all trauma patients
Treatment Steps:
1. DO NOT move the patient unless in immediate danger
2. Keep head, neck, and spine perfectly aligned
3. Apply cervical collar if available
4. Log-roll technique ONLY if must be moved
5. Maintain airway without tilting head

SECTION 2: BLEEDING AND WOUND CARE
=====================================

2.1 SEVERE BLEEDING PROTOCOL
------------------------------
Severity Assessment:
- CRITICAL: Arterial bleeding (bright red, spurting), loss of large volume
- HIGH: Venous bleeding (dark red, steady flow)
- MEDIUM: Capillary bleeding (slow ooze)

Treatment Steps:
1. Apply direct pressure using sterile gauze or clean cloth
2. Maintain firm, continuous pressure for minimum 10 minutes
3. Do NOT remove initial dressing - add more gauze on top if soaked
4. For limb bleeding: apply tourniquet 2-3 inches above wound if direct pressure fails
5. Mark tourniquet application time on patient's skin
6. Elevate bleeding limb above heart level if no fracture suspected
7. Watch for signs of shock (pale, cold, sweaty skin; rapid weak pulse)
8. Keep patient warm and lying down
9. Do NOT apply tourniquet to neck, armpit, or groin

Tourniquet Application:
1. Position tourniquet 2-3 inches above wound
2. Tighten until bleeding stops
3. Record time of application prominently
4. Never loosen once applied in field

2.2 MINOR CUT AND LACERATION PROTOCOL
---------------------------------------
Treatment Steps:
1. Clean hands before treating wound
2. Apply gentle pressure with clean cloth
3. Clean wound with clean water or saline
4. Apply antiseptic solution
5. Cover with sterile bandage or dressing
6. Change dressing daily or if soaked

SECTION 3: BURNS
==================

3.1 THERMAL BURN PROTOCOL
---------------------------
Burn Degree Classification:
- First Degree: Redness, no blisters (superficial)
- Second Degree: Blisters, wet appearance (partial thickness)
- Third Degree: White/charred, no pain (full thickness) - CRITICAL

Treatment Steps:
1. Remove from heat source immediately (ensure your safety first)
2. Remove clothing and jewelry near burn (if not stuck to skin)
3. Cool burn with cool (NOT cold/iced) running water for 20 minutes minimum
4. Do NOT use ice, butter, toothpaste, or home remedies
5. Cover loosely with sterile non-stick dressing or cling wrap
6. Do NOT burst blisters
7. Apply burn gel if available
8. Give pain relief for first/second degree burns
9. Keep patient warm (risk of hypothermia)
10. Third degree burns - cover only, no cooling - seek emergency help immediately

For Chemical Burns:
1. Remove contaminated clothing
2. Flush with large amounts of water for 20+ minutes
3. Do NOT try to neutralize the chemical

3.2 ELECTRICAL BURN PROTOCOL
------------------------------
CRITICAL - Do NOT touch victim if still in contact with electricity
Treatment Steps:
1. Disconnect power source or use non-conductive tool to move victim
2. Check for breathing and pulse - CPR may be needed
3. Treat burns as thermal burns
4. Assume internal injuries always exist with electrical burns
5. Cardiac monitoring required - risk of arrhythmia

SECTION 4: CARDIAC EMERGENCIES
================================

4.1 CARDIAC ARREST PROTOCOL
-----------------------------
Signs: Unresponsive, not breathing, no pulse

Treatment Steps (Chain of Survival):
1. Check scene safety
2. Check responsiveness - tap shoulders, shout "Are you okay?"
3. Call for emergency services immediately
4. Begin CPR:
   - Position: Heel of hands on center of chest
   - Depth: At least 2 inches
   - Rate: 100-120 compressions per minute
   - Ratio: 30 compressions : 2 rescue breaths (or hands-only CPR)
5. Use AED/Defibrillator as soon as available:
   - Turn on AED
   - Attach pads as shown in diagram
   - Ensure no one touching patient during shock
   - Resume CPR immediately after shock
6. Continue until patient recovers or emergency services arrive

4.2 HEART ATTACK PROTOCOL
---------------------------
Signs: Chest pain, left arm/jaw pain, sweating, nausea, shortness of breath

Treatment Steps:
1. Help patient sit or lie in comfortable position (semi-reclined)
2. Loosen tight clothing
3. Give aspirin (325mg) if not allergic and not contraindicated
4. Provide oxygen if available
5. Monitor vital signs every 5 minutes
6. Prepare for CPR if patient loses consciousness
7. Do NOT leave patient alone

SECTION 5: RESPIRATORY EMERGENCIES
=====================================

5.1 CHOKING PROTOCOL
---------------------
Conscious Adult:
1. Encourage coughing
2. Give 5 back blows between shoulder blades
3. Give 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver)
4. Alternate back blows and abdominal thrusts
5. If unconscious - begin CPR

5.2 ASTHMA ATTACK PROTOCOL
----------------------------
Treatment Steps:
1. Help patient use their own inhaler
2. Sit upright, lean slightly forward
3. Remove triggers (smoke, pets, etc.)
4. Encourage slow, controlled breathing
5. Give bronchodilator inhaler: 4 puffs, 4 breaths each, repeat every 4 minutes
6. If no improvement after 4 puffs - seek emergency help

SECTION 6: DISASTER-SPECIFIC PROTOCOLS
=========================================

6.1 FLOOD EMERGENCY PROTOCOL
------------------------------
Priority Actions:
1. Move to highest available ground
2. Do NOT walk in moving water (6 inches can knock an adult down)
3. Do NOT drive through flooded areas
4. Treat for hypothermia if wet
5. Disinfect all wounds (floodwater is highly contaminated)
6. Treat drinking water before consumption

Rescue Operations:
1. Use rope for water rescue - do NOT enter fast-moving water
2. Throw rescue equipment - reach, throw, don't go
3. Check for structural damage before entering buildings
4. Watch for electrical hazards (downed lines, flooded electrical panels)

6.2 EARTHQUAKE PROTOCOL
------------------------
During Earthquake:
1. DROP, COVER, HOLD ON
2. Under sturdy table or desk
3. Away from windows and exterior walls

After Earthquake:
1. Check for injuries using TRIAGE protocol
2. Check for gas leaks (do NOT use flames)
3. Check for structural damage
4. Prepare for aftershocks
5. Do NOT use elevators

6.3 FIRE EMERGENCY PROTOCOL
-----------------------------
Evacuation:
1. Activate fire alarm
2. Low crawl if smoke present
3. Check doors for heat before opening
4. Close doors behind you
5. Never use elevator
6. Assembly at designated muster point

For Trapped Victims:
1. Seal gaps under doors with cloth
2. Signal from windows
3. Stay low
4. Do NOT open windows if smoke outside

SECTION 7: TRIAGE MASS CASUALTY PROTOCOL
==========================================

START Triage System:
- IMMEDIATE (Red): Life-threatening, can survive with immediate intervention
- DELAYED (Yellow): Serious but stable, can wait
- MINOR (Green): Walking wounded, minimal care needed
- EXPECTANT (Black): Unsurvivable injuries or deceased

Triage Assessment Order:
1. Breathing - is patient breathing?
2. Perfusion - capillary refill < 2 seconds?
3. Mental status - can follow simple commands?

SECTION 8: ENVIRONMENTAL EMERGENCIES
=======================================

8.1 HYPOTHERMIA PROTOCOL
--------------------------
Signs: Shivering, confusion, slurred speech, pale/blue skin

Treatment Steps:
1. Move to warm, dry environment
2. Remove wet clothing
3. Cover with blankets (include head)
4. Give warm (not hot) beverages if conscious
5. Do NOT rub or massage extremities
6. Handle gently - risk of cardiac arrhythmia
7. Monitor core temperature if possible

8.2 HEAT STROKE PROTOCOL
--------------------------
Signs: High body temperature (>104°F/40°C), confusion, hot dry skin, no sweating

Treatment Steps:
1. CRITICAL - Cool immediately
2. Move to cool environment
3. Remove excess clothing
4. Ice packs to neck, armpits, and groin
5. Wet skin and fan vigorously
6. Cool water immersion if available
7. Do NOT give oral fluids if unconscious
8. Monitor temperature every 5 minutes
